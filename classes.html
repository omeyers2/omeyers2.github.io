UW Courses, Professors, and the B-School

School of Business at UW

The B-School at UW offers its students so many amazing features and benefits. From tutoring to business student orgs to study spaces, being a business student at UW provides you with amazing opportunities and advantages. The school offers mock interviews, career fairs, cover page and resume help, advising resources, etc. I feel supported and confident navigating through my college career into my future professional one.

My Advice:

  • Get to know your professors and TAs! Answer questions in class, introduce yourself after class or in an email, go to office hours, add them on LinkedIn, etc.
  • Go to office hours/ask for help if you need it! I did not take advantage of this enough my first semester freshman year. This is a great way to form those relationships with your professors and TAs and is incredibly helpful when you're struggling with any of the class material.
  • Go to the library or a coffee shop to study. In my opinion, studying in your dorm room or apartment, where you sleep, eat, and relax, is tough and at times, distracting. Studying in another environment that promotes learning helps me be more productive and focused!
  • Join study groups and student orgs! They are super helpful and a great way to make new friends in your classes. I am so happy that I have met so many unique students in different classes throughout my college career so far.
  • Make appointments and get help from your advisor. UW's advising services are great resources on campus to help guide you through your college experience.
  • Have a schedule and stay organized!
  • Have an open mind to all subjects, courses, people, professors, ideas, etc! You will be pleasantly surprised by how many new things you will be exposed to and get the chance to experience, so approach them with open arms! You may find new passions and interests you never even knew about before getting to UW.
